Jérome Ricciardi

Formal methods, OCaml tooling, and quantum software

I am a software engineer and applied researcher with a background in formal methods and quantum software. My work focuses on tool development, program and circuit verification, and user-facing application support for scientific computing platforms.

I have worked on deductive verification, symbolic execution, OCaml tooling, and verification techniques for quantum and hybrid quantum circuits.

Current work

Application Support Engineer at Quandela, working on Lucy, Quandela's quantum computer deployed at TGCC. I contributed to its integration into the computing center. I support users on the application and algorithmic side, and I act as a liaison between Quandela and TGCC.

Research / PhD

I defended my PhD thesis, Practical Verification of Quantum Circuit Transformations, on 4 June 2026 at École normale supérieure Paris-Saclay.

The thesis focused on deductive verification, equivalence checking for quantum and hybrid circuits, circuit transformations, and tool development for formal verification workflows.

Projects

Thesis projects

  • Qbricks to OpenQASM

    Why3 development for certifying a Qbricks-to-OpenQASM translation pipeline. The work proves semantics-preserving rewrites that eliminate or compile Qbricks constructs before generating OpenQASM code.

  • SQbricks

    Tool chain for automatically checking hybrid quantum circuit transformations. It combines deferred-measurement lifting with observed-output equivalence and separation checks to handle measurements, classical control, and discarded data.

Master projects

  • AutoCheck

    Master's project on automated testing and checking of OCaml and WhyML properties.

  • ENUM

    Master's project related to enumeration-based testing and property checking.

Quandela / related work
